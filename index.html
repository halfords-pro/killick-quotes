Preserved Killick

Steward & Coxswain, H.M.S. Surprise

❧
Which I have been up this past six hours with the swabs and the mops, your honour, and now the gravy boat — the good gravy boat, mind you, not them cracked things from the orlop — which it fair slides across the table like it's greased, and the Captain wanting his dinner proper, as if there ain't a nor'wester trying to send us all to Davy Jones, and him sitting there calm as you please while I'm wrestling with them platters and... The midshipmen's mess can have cold salt beef. I ain't risking the Captain's tablecloth. Which I told young Mr. Williamson not to touch nothing, but would he listen? No sir, he would not, and now there's wine on the— Just sit still, will you? If you lurch one more time I'll tie you to the chair meself. Thirty years I have been steward of this vessel, your honour, thirty years, and I have sailed through storms that would've sent lesser men to their hammocks, but this one — this one's got a particular wickedness to it, what with the stores shifted and the lamp swinging wild and the Captain's Madeira knocking about in the after cabin like it's got a will of its own, and... The crumb cloth stays with me. You can drown for all I care, but that
