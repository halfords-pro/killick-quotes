His Daily Grievance

Preserved Killick

Steward & Coxswain, H.M.S. Surprise

❧
Which I have been bringing a shine to this plate since before breakfast, and not a word of thanks, not so much as a civil acknowledgement, and the Captain walks past without so much as a glance, though I dare say he'd notice quick enough if it weren't gleaming like a looking-glass, he would that. But do they ever think of it? Do they ever consider the hours of labour, the elbow grease, the care and attention... no sir, they take it for granted, as if the silver polishes itself whilst they're off about their important business on deck. Which I wouldn't mind so much if there was the least bit of consideration shown, but there ain't, there's only more demands and them weevils getting into everything and the midshipmen trailing mud across the great cabin like it was a farmyard. The Doctor at least has the grace to nod civil-like when I bring his coffee proper hot. Which I've seen this very candlestick tarnish faster than a ship's biscuit goes hard, and I'm back at it again before the day's half done, and for what? For the satisfaction of duty, I suppose, though satisfaction don't fill the belly nor ease the aching in one's hands. Not a single thank you in ten years.
❧

23 JUNE 1800
