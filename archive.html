Previous Grievances

Steward & Coxswain, H.M.S. Surprise

10 JUNE 1800

Which I could of wept when I found that there glass laying about in the gunroom like a common object, all smudged with fingerprints and salt spray, them young gentlemen treating it like it were a... a piece of cordage or some such trash that don't signify. The Captain's own telescope, which he brought aboard personal and which cost more than them midshipmen's wages in a twelvemonth, and there it lay without so much as a cloth to protect it or a by-your-leave from anyone with the authority to... and I'll have to polish it again, won't I, because that's how things proceed on this vessel. No one thinks to ask old Killick. Not a word. Which they'd have their ears boxed proper if the Captain were to find out they'd had his glass without permission, and serve 'em right. I've half a mind to say nothing and let the blame fall where it will, but I suppose I'll have to... no, I suppose I shall.

❧

9 JUNE 1800

Which I have swabbed this deck not two hours past, and now here's the water coming in again like we're at the bottom of the sea instead of sailing upon it, and the Captain's dinner going cold on the table — the best cloth too, which I laid out special — and them young midshipmen tracking salt water through here every moment like they was born in a barn, and the gravy boat near sliding off... your honour, if you would just sit still for one moment instead of pacing about like that, it would be considerably easier to keep anything dry in this floating... well. Which it cannot be done, sir. Not when the very timbers are weeping. I'll have the swabs out again soon as this passes, your honour, and the cabin will be decent for your breakfast, you may depend upon it — though what rest I'm to get is a question I've long since stopped asking, seeing as how the answer is never. Beg pardon, sir, but that water's coming faster on the starboard side. Which I shall want fresh linens, and the Captain's good towels... Three days I've had my boots dry. Three.

❧

8 JUNE 1800

Which I have been polishing this here silver for nigh on twenty years, and not a soul has ever said "Killick, that's a handsome piece of work" or offered me so much as an extra ration of grog, and now the midshipmen are expected to know the difference between the fish forks and the dinner forks without so much as a by-your-leave, and the Captain himself wouldn't notice if I polished it with his own coat... Not that I'm looking for thanks, mind you. Which I do my duty because it's my duty, and them candlesticks won't polish themselves no matter how much the Doctor carries on about idle hands... Unbelievable, that's what it is. Absolutely unbelievable. Which I have half a mind to let Bonden's boy have a go at it next time, see how he manages when the tarnish sets in proper, though I won't of course because standards must be maintained and the Captain deserves better than... Thank you. Ha. As if.

❧

7 JUNE 1800

Which I have not slept this three nights, your honour, on account of them rats — the very rats that has got into the Captain's preserved ginger, the good sort from Canton, the sort what cannot be replaced this side of the Cape, and now there is naught but the crumbs and the wrappings scattered about like... like them worthless midshipmen after they have been at the jam. And the Captain will be wanting his ginger for his digestion, and what am I to tell him? That some black-hearted vermin has made off with it whilst I was attending to the boatswain's linens? There is no justice in this world, none at all... Rats, says I. As if rats was the natural order of things aboard a ship. Well they ain't natural to my pantry, I can tell you that much. Which I shall have to set them traps again, and stay awake the livelong night listening for the snap of them, like some sort of... like some poor soul condemned... Them rats has cost me more sleep than a full gale. Which I will lay poison, I will, and if the Captain's cat has not the sense to do her duty, then perhaps the cat ought to be eating less of the ship's stores and catching more of the vermin what eats 'em.

❧

6 JUNE 1800

Which I ain't had me head on a pillow since Tuesday last, your honour—or was it Monday?—and now there's toasted cheese wanted, and never mind that them galley fires is scarce fit for boiling water, let alone the delicate application of heat what toasted cheese requires, and the best cheese what we had is near gone, ate up by them weevils or the Doctor's experiments, I never can tell which, and all this on top of the Captain wanting his Madeira just so, with the proper decanter, and Mr Pullings asking for breakfast at all hours, and the mess to be tidied, and— Which I'll have it ready in ten minutes, your honour, though it'll be the death of me.

5 JUNE 1800

Them midshipmen left the best Madeira glasses on the quarter-deck. There's weevils in the biscuits again. 4 JUNE 1800

Which I have been saving this cloth these eighteen months, ever since we put into Port Royal, and now some careless swab has gone and... and there is a stain the size of a man's hand right through the damask, and I don't suppose anyone will own to it, not a soul, they'll all look at their shoes and shuffle about like they never saw such a thing in their lives, and here I am with naught but cold water and the Captain expecting gentlemen to dinner tonight, cold water and a prayer to... Them that spill wine and don't report it ought to be keelhauled, that's what I say.

❧

3 JUNE 1800

Which I laid out them bottles myself not three days past, and now there's near a full inch gone from the Captain's private stock, which it is a hanging matter if he discovers it, and here I am blamed for every drop that goes missing in this ship, as though I am not run off me feet already with them weevils in the biscuit and the new midshipman what cannot tell a tablecloth from a dish rag... Begging your pardon, sir, but if I was to find out who has been at them bottles, I would personally see they was clapped in irons before breakfast.

❧

2 JUNE 1800

Which I have heard better sounds from them weevils in the biscuit, and I'll say no more about it, for the Captain values the Doctor's learning, but Lord love us, them notes coming through the canvas... it fair sets the teeth on edge, your honour would say so if he had the courage, but loyalty's a thing that must be borne... *pauses while wiping a plate* The gun crews make less noise practising their drill, which is saying something.

❧

1 JUNE 1800

Which I have had this coat near five years and never a spot nor crease where it shouldn't be, but now — now when the Captain's wanting to look his best before they come aboard, them midshipmen have got at it somehow, got chocolate or worse all over the collar, and I'm stood here with a flat iron that ain't hot enough, and even if it was I'd burn the lace sure as eggs, and there's no time, there's never no time, which is always the way of it... The iron's cold as charity. Which I could have had this pressed proper yesternight if someone — and I won't name names but it rhymes with Bonden — hadn't been larking about and spilling his grog all over the starboard locker where I keeps the pressing board, so now I've got to do the whole thing standing on me knees with a flat stone from the galley, which is hardly the way to treat a captain's coat, hardly respectful, but nobody thinks of Preserved Killick and what he's got to put up with, do they, your honour... they just expects miracles... Them irons needs heating proper. Needs a proper fire. Needs thanks what never comes.

❧

31 MAY 1800

Which I would serve the coffee direct, your honour, only the mill's been broke these three days and not a soul has seen fit to mention it to me, though I've got eyes like a hawk and hands that can fix near anything, but does anyone come asking Killick what he needs? No sir, they do not. They just sit there expecting their morning cup like it grows on trees, when the fact is I've got to pound them beans with a hammer like some ship's carpenter, and the noise of it... Christ, the noise of it brings the Captain down wondering what's what, and then I'm the one gets the eye when it's not my doing at all but them who let the mill go to rack and ruin in the first place...

❧

30 MAY 1800

Which I have told Captain this a hundred times if I've told him once, but do he listen? No, your honour, he do not. Them weevils is got into the biscuit again — and not just a few neither, but thick as flies on a butcher's block — which it is a scandal and a disgrace to a vessel of His Majesty's Navy, it is. I opens the barrel this morning all innocent-like, thinking to lay out a nice selection for the Captain's breakfast, and there they was, the little black devils, thick as ... well, there's no call to go on about it, but the long and the short of it is that I shall have to sift the whole lot through muslin, and my back ain't what it was, and nobody gives a fig for poor Preserved Killick... Weevils again. Which I should of known better than to expect anything better from the stores at Port Royal — them lazy lubbers wouldn't know a proper victual if it walked up and bit 'em.

❧

29 MAY 1800

Which I have been to every blessed dairy from here to the market and not a drop of proper cream to be had, not a single drop, and them saying Oh we have milk, sir, we have butter, but cream? Which they look at me like I've asked for the crown jewels, and all the while the Captain's coffee sits there getting cold and thin as ditch water, and what am I supposed to tell him? That there ain't no cream in this godforsaken port? That the Doctor's eaten it all up with his puddings... *shuffles away muttering* Thirty years I've been keeping that man's breakfast proper, and now I'm to be defeated by a bit of cream.

❧

28 MAY 1800

Which I have scarce finished scrubbing the orlop from stem to stern, what with them midshipmen trailing their filthy rope-yarns about like they was born in a barn, and now I am to conjure up passenger berths from the very air itself... The Captain's Madeira will need to be moved, naturally, and them provisions what I have took such pains to stow proper will want rearranging, and I shall have no sleep again this night, none whatsoever... More passengers. As if this vessel was not crowded enough with the standing officers and all. Which I dare say the Doctor will have some notion of doctoring them, but who is to see they have fresh water and clean sheets and their necessaries—which I will tell you plain, it will be me, and not a word of thanks for it neither. Them passengers, they always want the best berth, the warmest corner, fresh air, proper light—which I am to provide all of it on a ship that is already more packed than a merchant's hold full of spices. Which if the Captain had given me proper notice, your honour, I might have made some arrangement, but here we are with the tide turning and them berths still wanting...

❧

27 MAY 1800

Which I have mended these particular stockings no less than seven times, and still the Captain goes through 'em like they was made of spider's web, wear at the heel something chronic, and never a word of thanks, just Captain wants his best black silk for the dinner with the Governor, Captain wants them without a visible darn, Captain wants... and here I am with darning needle at three in the morning with my eyes gone crooked and not a soul to appreciate the neatness of the work, which is museum-quality, your honour, museum-quality, but do they notice? They do not. Them stockings is like the Captain himself — seems indestructible till you look close and find they're worn through at every critical point. Which I recall when he was a midshipman, his mother used to send fresh stockings by the dozen, and I dare say he thought they grew on trees, the way he wore through 'em then, and nothing has changed but now it falls to me to keep him presentable and the ship's stores getting lower every month, and I've had to unravel an old waistcoat just to find decent thread, which is... One more darn and they'll be more thread than stocking, mark my words.

❧

26 MAY 1800

Which I would not have believed it if I had not seen it with me own eyes, them young gentlemen passing the Captain's best telescope about like it was a common spy-glass from the slops chest, and not a word to your honour, not so much as a by-your-leave, and if that glass gets so much as a smudge upon it there will be the devil to pay, and I will be the one to pay it, which is the way of things in this vessel and always has been... Them midshipmen will answer for this, mark my words. Which I polish that telescope every morning before the Captain takes his breakfast, every morning without fail these five years and more, and now it is gone walking about the deck like it belongs to the whole ship instead of residing safe in the Captain's quarters where it ought to be... Little blighters. Which if the Captain hears of this there will be trouble of the sort that makes grown men weep, and them youngsters will be scrubbing the heads until their hands are raw, and serve 'em right too, for there is no respect aboard this vessel any more, none at all, and a man cannot turn his back for two minutes without the whole ship falling into chaos and disorder...

❧

25 MAY 1800

Which I have been up since before the middle watch trying to get them ports sealed proper, and now here comes his honour expecting his dinner on a decent cloth—not them stained ones, mind you, the good damask what I had laid out not two hours since—and the ship pitching like a drunken Portuguese, and me with barely two hands to my name and the water coming in through every crack God ever made in oak... The soup's cold, which cannot be helped. Which if them midshipmen had done their duty instead of gawping at the horizon like a parcel of landlubbers, we might have had them gratings fitted before the barometer fell through the deck, but no, it's always left to old Killick to manage, with his joints screaming and his back... There, there, sir, mind your step, that's the third puddle just this side of the table... Not a soul asks if Preserved Killick has had his breakfast, or his dinner either. The Captain's Madeira is safe, at any rate. Which is something, though it's cost me the better part of the forenoon watch and a bruise on my shin that'll turn black as a cannonball before supper time...

❧

24 MAY 1800

Which I have been at this plate since before the watch changed, and not a soul has remarked upon it—not the Captain, though he'll notice quick enough if there's a spot, which there won't be, and not the Doctor neither, though he drinks his coffee from it near enough every morning, and these poor hands raw from the buffing cloth, and the canvas bag I brought up from the hold positively writhing with them weevils, so that I had to... There now. Spotless. As it always is. As it will always be, whether or no the Captain so much as glances at it. Which is the way of things, I suppose, and no use complaining, though complain I shall if I'm moved to it... Not a word of thanks in all the service. Which I daresay if he knew what these forks had cost me—the labour of it, the materials—he'd think different. But officers don't think, not about such matters. They think about winds and tides and the enemy, and some of us must think about keeping their silver fit to grace the table of a gentleman. The weevils will eat anything, given time.

❧

23 MAY 1800

Which I have took every precaution known to Christian man—sealed boxes, tar paper, the lot—and still them vermin finds their way in like they owns the ship. The Captain's preserved ginger, sir, his very own ginger what he likes with his coffee, now gnawed through and half of it gone to rat droppings and... and I dare not show me face below without some explanation what will satisfy, which it won't, nothing satisfies when a man has worked himself to the bone trying to keep order amongst... Them rats. Them bleeding rats.

❧

22 MAY 1800

Which I've been on me feet for near forty hours, your honour, and now it's toasted cheese, is it... Toasted cheese when I haven't had me head on a pillow since Tuesday last, and that for no more than a cat's nap before the Doctor came banging on the galley door wanting beef tea for some midshipman with the belly-ache. Toasted cheese! As if the stove weren't hot enough already with them weevils in the biscuit and the Captain wanting his breakfast at all hours and now you gentlemen must have your cheese toasted, must you, when there's a hundred things want doing and me own eyes like sandpaper... Which I'll do it, course I will, but there'll be a reckoning when I fall down dead in the scuppers, mark my words. Toasted cheese when a man's dead on his feet—what's next, I wonder? Dancing the hornpipe?

❧

21 MAY 1800

Which I only just got them candlesticks polished proper, and now there's visitors coming this very afternoon and the Captain's best coat wants pressing and there ain't a clean tablecloth to be had, the last one being still damp from when the Doctor spilt his claret like a careless midshipman, and now they're telling me short notice, as if I ain't got enough on my plate without... without... That's always the way of it, ain't it. Nobody thinks to tell old Killick till the last possible moment. Which I shall have to turn them cushions, your honour, and air out the cabin proper, and I do hope as you ain't expecting miracles, because miracles require time, and time is the one thing we ain't got, seeing as how Captain don't believe in giving a man proper warning of these things. The best Madeira's still in the hold. It'll take me an hour to fetch it up, and another hour to let it settle proper, and by then... Aye, well, they'll just have to make do with what they gets, won't they. Which is more than fair, considering.

❧

20 MAY 1800

Which I could weep, your honour, I could fairly weep. That there stain weren't there this morning, I'd stake me life on it, and now look at it — spreading like them weevils in the biscuit, which I'll never get it out, never in a month of Sundays, not with all the fresh water in the Indian Ocean and working me fingers to the bone... Blast and damn. Which I suppose it was them midshipmen larking about with their wine or some such, them thoughtless young devils what wouldn't know a proper cloth from a dish rag if it came up and introduced itself, and the Captain expecting everything just so for dinner tonight, and here's me with naught but this ruined thing and... The second best cloth. That'll have to do.

❧

19 MAY 1800

Which I should like to know, sir, which I should indeed, who has been tampering with them bottles in the Captain's cabin, for there's a level as plain as the nose on your face gone down near half an inch, and the Captain particular about his Madeira, very particular, and now I shall have to answer for it when he notices—and he will notice, being a gentleman of discernment—and there I'll be caught between the Devil and the deep, made to look a fool or worse when I've done nothing but keep them stores locked tight as I always do... Though I've me suspicions, which I won't name, but there's certain young gentlemen with more thirst than sense...

❧

18 MAY 1800

Which I have respect for a learned gentleman, your honour, but that there instrument sounds like a tomcat in considerable distress, and if the Captain's mood goes sour on account of them dreadful scrapings, it will be Killick who bears the brunt of it, not Doctor Maturin with his head full of beetles and Latin, oh no... Them that can't play ought not to play where Christian ears might hear it. Which I've heard the gun-crews manage better music with their tampions and swabs, I have truly, and that's saying something...

❧

17 MAY 1800

Which I have told them a hundred times if I have told them once, this coat requires the utmost delicacy, and here is young Watts tramping about the orlop like a bull in a china shop, letting the damp get into everything... The Captain's dress coat, his best coat, the one as was cleaned and pressed particular for the Admiral's dinner in Port Royal, and now I must do it all over again because some fool has left the scuttle open to the weather and the salt air has got at it... There will be not a word of thanks, you understand, not a breath of acknowledgement when his honour steps into that cabin and finds it all neat as a new pin, ready for him to put on without so much as a wrinkle to disturb him, and meanwhile I have not had my breakfast these two hours... A coat like that does not press itself, sir. Which I swear by all that is holy, if that midshipman comes near this cabin one more time with his muddy feet I shall have something to say, and the Doctor will not like it much when he is called to...

❧

16 MAY 1800

Which I would serve the coffee this very moment, sir, if them midshipmen hadn't left the mill in such a state as to render it... well, which it won't grind now, will it? Not a grain. And there's the Captain wanting his coffee, and me with a handful of beans and not a blessed thing to do with 'em but pound 'em with the back of a spoon like some savage in the Pacific, which is hardly the thing for a proper steward, is it? The Doctor can have his tea, at least, but him that commands the Surprise expects his coffee at a civilized hour, and now... Which I'll manage it somehow, your honour, I always do.

❧

15 MAY 1800

Which I have seen them weevils before, your honour, seen 'em a thousand times these twenty years at sea, but never — and I do mean *never* — have I encountered such a pestilential infestation as what is currently infesting them biscuits in the hold. Them things is not merely living in the bread, sir, they have *established themselves*, set up a whole society down there, breeding and multiplying like the very devil himself had took an interest in the victualling stores... The Captain shall have his decent biscuit notwithstanding, which I shall personally inspect every morsel before it touches his table, but as for the rest of the ship's company, well, they shall have to make do with what God and the Admiralty saw fit to provide, weevils and all. Disgusting creatures. Which it stands to reason that a man cannot keep them things out entirely when the very fabric of the ship sweats and weeps with damp, and the biscuits has been aboard since Portsmouth, but still — a little care in the stowage would not have gone amiss, would it? Which I have not had a moment's sleep this past night, nor will I have another until every biscuit has been gone through, one by one, and... Vermin.

❧

14 MAY 1800

Which I have been to every blessed dairy and chandlery from here to the marketplace, your honour would not believe the state of things, not a drop of proper cream to be had, nothing but that thin blue milk that a cat would spit at, and the Captain taking his coffee at oh-five-thirty as regular as the tides, and here I am with my reputation on the line... Them dairymaids don't know cream from bilge water, I swear it. Which I might as well be asking for the Crown Jewels, the way these people carry on about cream as though it were liquid gold, when all I need is a single pot of decent stuff, thick and yellow, not this... this watered nonsense they're trying to palm off on me... The Doctor don't help none, going on about the milk being perfectly wholesome, but he never has to face the Captain when his coffee tastes like disappointment, do he? Which I shall have to try the garrison stores tomorrow at first light, though that Mrs Henderson there is meaner than a pressed rat and likely to charge me double for the trouble of it... Lost a night's sleep over this already.

❧

13 MAY 1800

Which I only just got them cabins proper aired and the mattresses turned, and now there's more of them coming aboard, which the Captain didn't see fit to mention it till this very morning, and Mr Pullings standing there casual-like with his list of names and requirements, as if I haven't got enough on me plate... More passengers means more fussing, more demands for fresh water and hot towels at all hours, and them young ladies from before barely taught to stow their petticoats proper. Which I shall have to— but there's no use complaining, is there, the Captain's word is law and I wouldn't have it otherwise, only a bit of notice would've been civil. Them midshipmen will be sleeping four to a space now, which they'll not like and I'll hear all about it, though whose fault is that I wonder... Which the best tablecloth will be wanted, I suppose, and there'll be cream for coffee that I shall have to coax from the cook, and extra polishing of the silver, and— and all without a soul to help worth a farthing. Not as if I've had a proper sleep in three days. The Captain's Madeira will need a close eye kept on it, mark my words.

❧

12 MAY 1800

Which I have been mending these stockings since before breakfast, your honour would wear through em like a man possessed, and these was only darned not three weeks back... holes in the heels, holes in the toes, and I don't know how a gentleman can contrive to go through good worsted at such a rate unless he's walking on broken glass, which I wouldn't put past him given the way he tears about the deck in a blow... and where's the thanks? Where's the recognition that these hands of mine have kept this ship's captain decent and respectable, with not a thread showing, not a darn visible to the eye of anyone... but no, it's just more stockings, more work, more hours lost from my proper duties... Them midshipmen don't go through half what the Captain does. Which I shall need to put in for more yarn, and the purser will say there's none to be had, and then what am I to do, send him out naked from the ankle to the knee like a... Not a whisper of gratitude in this service, not one.

❧

11 MAY 1800

Which I would like to know how they come to think they can just walk into the great cabin and... and take his honour's things as though this were some sort of common vessel with common discipline, instead of a proper ship under a proper captain. Them young gentlemen, they got no more sense than a bosun's call, the lot of 'em. And now his honour will be wanting his telescope when he comes on deck, and I'll be the one getting the black look, though I had nothing to do with it—nothing whatsoever—and I'll have to search the whole bloody ship to find where they've left it, probably with the lens all fogged up from their greasy fingers and... Which I wouldn't be surprised if one of them already dropped it. That's young Mr. Hollom for you, always sneaking about after his betters. Or them other two—no more discipline than a parrot on a perch. His honour gives 'em every kindness and how does it repay him? Borrowed instruments. Borrowed! As if asking polite-like would be too much trouble when you're meant to learn your trade. And not a word of notice to me. Not a by-your-leave.

❧

10 MAY 1800

Which I have been at this since the middle watch, sopping and wiping and moving them candlesticks so they don't go sliding across the table like... like they was skating on ice, your honour, and now the cook sends up this gravy what's been sloshing about in the galley till it's half seawater and half sense, and I'm meant to serve it up pretending it's fit for a Christian, let alone the Captain, which it ain't, and every time the ship takes a roll I'm down on me knees again with the... with the... Bonden could help, but no, he's up on deck being useful. Which I have wore a hole in me left stocking from kneeling, and that's the third bucket I've filled today, and the Doctor come down here not an hour since looking for his lint and never once asked how I was managing with this... this tempest... The tablecloth's ruined. That was the good Madeira damask. Which that midshipman Hollom, he tracked water through here not ten minutes ago, and did he think to wipe his feet? Did he indeed.

❧

9 MAY 1800

Which I have been polishing of this here silver since before the middle watch, my fingers raw as a ship's biscuit, and not so much as a civil word from anyone, not the Captain—begging your pardon, your honour—not the Captain when he comes into the cabin, not the Doctor, not them midshipmen what trail their grubby mitts across the table when they think I ain't looking... Ungrateful set of hogs. Which I could take it if they was just careless-like, but they ain't—they're deliberate. They see me polish this candlestick till it shines like the morning star, and do they remark upon it? Do they say, Killick, that's a fine bit of work? No sir. They do not. They just take it for granted, the way they take the coffee that ain't half stewed, the way they take clean stockings and a proper mess kit laid out... And all the while my back's killing me and the lamp oil's got in under my nails something fierce... Which it ain't the silver that troubles me so much as the ingratitude of it all, if you take my meaning, sir.

❧

8 MAY 1800

Which I have took such care with them jars, such particular care, and now look at 'em — nibbled they are, gnawed right through the paper and the wax both, the little vermin... The Captain do set such store by his preserved ginger, very particular about his preserved ginger he is, and here I got to tell him as how them rats have been at it. Not that it's my fault, mark you, for I keep a clean berth, which I do, but there it is — them rats, them cursed rats will have their way no matter what a man might do, and I'll get the blame for it as sure as I'm standing here... Them rats ought to be drowned. Which I wonder what the Doctor would say if I was to let spoilage get at his wine and his particulars — wouldn't hear the end of it, I wouldn't, and the Captain's ginger is near as precious to him as his Madeira, which is saying something considerable... The Captain won't be pleased. And who has got to go down to that dark hold with a candle and a cudgel to deal with 'em? Which I'll tell you who — which it's me, Preserved Killick, what has got a hundred other things wanting doing this very minute, and no thanks for any of it...

❧

7 MAY 1800

Which I haven't closed my eyes since Wednesday forenoon watch, your honour, and now it's toasted cheese, as though the Captain don't know perfectly well that them cheeses what came aboard in Madeira is sweating like... like... and here's me meant to stand over it with the toasting fork without dropping asleep into the flames...

❧

6 MAY 1800

Which I never had no warning, not a moment's notice to get them candlesticks proper, and the Captain only tells me now when there's scarce time to draw breath, and I'm meant to work miracles with what little help I get from them useless swabs in the orlop... Them visitors don't know the half of it. They'll see a neat cabin and a tidy table and the finest Madeira poured without a splash, and they won't think for a moment about poor Preserved Killick up half the night, polishing and scrubbing and arranging, when I should've been... well, when I should've been getting some proper rest for once in my life. The best tablecloth's damp still. Which I tell you, your honour, if the Captain wanted visitors he might've had the courtesy to mention it before breakfast, and then perhaps I could've... Which I do all the work aboard this vessel and no one knows it, and if something goes wrong, well, then they knows it quick enough, your honour gets told directly, and Killick gets the rough edge of the tongue. But when everything's proper and shipshape and running like clockwork, well, that's just as it should be, ain't it, nobody needs thanking for doing their duty... Not even a cup of decent coffee this morning.

22 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I took them tins up myself not three days ago, and them rats—them great whiskered vermin—they've gnawed clean through the copper and got at every last piece, and now what am I to say to his honour when he calls for his ginger with his coffee, which he particular likes it, and there's nothing for it but shame and... The Doctor won't be best pleased neither, seeing as he had designs on that ginger for his sick-bay preparations, or so he said, though likely he meant to filch a bit for himself, same as the rest of 'em. But that ain't the point. The point is them rats knew, they knew somehow, and they've made a right mess of the Captain's stores and there's nothing left but tins all holed and ginger spread about like... Nothing for it now but traps. Lots of traps. And no sleep.

21 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I haven't closed me eye since Tuesday last, your honour, and now you're wanting toasted cheese. Toasted cheese. As if there ain't the Captain's breakfast to lay out, and them weevils got into the ship's biscuit again, and the coffee's near gone because somebody — and I won't say names but he wears a coat with buttons — has been helping himself to the cream like it grows on trees. But no matter, I'll do your toasted cheese. I always do. Though how a man is meant to work the galley stove when he's dead on his feet and the cheese has gone hard as oakum, and I've got to find the good bread and not them mouldy ends... *trails off into muttering* The thanks I get for it, you could write on a grain of salt.

❧

20 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I only this moment finished swabbing of them boards, and now here comes word the Captain wants the great cabin made ready for them commodore and his suite, and I've scarce had time to draw breath, let alone fetch up them good glasses from the stores, and the tablecloth — which I had particular in mind the damask, the one without them spots — it's only just come from the laundry and ain't properly aired, and if there's one thing I cannot abide it's damp linen, it shows a want of respect, and now I'm to get fresh flowers, which flowers, your honour, I should like to know, when we're three weeks from any land with green things growing... Which I shall do it, mind you. The Captain's word is law, and them visitors shall find the cabin shipshape or I'll know the reason why. But it's the notice, sir — that's what grinds me. A man might accomplish wonders with time enough, but this... Where in God's name has young Oakes got to with them candlesticks?

❧

19 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I have took the greatest care with this cloth, your honour would not believe the labour, and now some villain has gone and... Lord, what is this? A stain? A stain! On the best damask, the very best, which I laid it out particular for them Portuguese ladies, and now it is ruined, utterly ruined, and not a soul aboard this barky gives a fig for the work that goes into keeping standards, not a single fig, and I shall have to... Christ, it will not come out, will it... That would be Bonden, mark my words. Or one of them young midshipmen with their filthy paws.

18 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I have been aboard this ship these twenty years and never seen such wickedness, which that Madeira cost the Captain's own money and it was decanted particular into them crystal glasses that I polished myself not two days since, and now some thieving article has... which I'll not say who, but there's one or two of them young gentlemen what thinks the world owes 'em a living, and if his honour finds out before I've had time to... well, there'll be the devil to pay and no mistake, and here's me without so much as a by-your-leave or a thank-you-kindly for keeping of his affairs in order.
