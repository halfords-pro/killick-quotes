Steward & Coxswain, H.M.S. Surprise

19 MARCH 1800

Which I don't profess to be no musician, your honour, but them sounds coming from the cabin just now... well, sir, I've heard worse from the galley cat when Chips trod on her tail, and that's saying something. The Doctor is a learned gentleman, begging your pardon, and I wouldn't speak ill of him for the world—him being so clever with his medicines and his Latin and his natural philosophy and all that—but which I've brought him his breakfast these twenty years and more, I've never heard nothing quite so... so... well, it fair curdled the cream in the Captain's coffee. Cats on a hot griddle, that's what it reminds me of. Which I suppose even great men must have their failings, and if the good Doctor wants to saw away at that there cello till the timbers shake and the salt junk cries out in protest, then who's Preserved Killick to say him nay? Not as though I've got anything else to do but mind my own business and ensure this ship runs proper... The midshipmen was laughing fit to split, the disrespectful young beasts.

18 MARCH 1800

Which I have had that coat these twenty years and never once has it come back from the gunroom with them midshipmen's grubby fingers all over the collar, and now I'm meant to get the creases sharp as a cutlass when the heat from the galley has made the very air thick as soup and the irons won't hold their temperature above a minute, not a minute, and the Captain expects it done by eight bells without so much as a by-your-leave... The lace on this coat, sir, which it took me a fortnight to restore after them youngsters got at it with their fooling about, and if that iron leaves so much as a scorch mark on the blue wool I shall... I shall... Only four midshipmen on this vessel and every one of 'em a disaster waiting to happen. Which I have pressed this very coat before the Governor himself, and his own steward — a man with proper facilities, mark you — couldn't have made a better job of it, not if he'd tried for a month. There. That'll do. Though it pains me to say anything's adequate in these conditions.

17 MARCH 1800

Which I would serve the coffee direct, sir, only them weevils has got into the beans something fierce, and the mill's busted clean through — the handle's gone all loose and the burrs won't turn proper, and there ain't no way to grind nothing, not without I stand there half the morning beating it with a marlinspike, which I got better things to do with my time, though no one seems to notice that, and the Captain wanting his coffee this very minute like he didn't know there was a war on... Broken mill. Which I could of told anyone it would come to this. Which I been saying for nigh on six months that mill needed attending to, but does anyone listen to old Killick? They do not. They just expect the coffee to appear, hot and proper, like it grows on trees... Sir.

16 MARCH 1800

Which I seen them weevils plain as day, your honour, right there in the Captain's stores, and didn't I warn Mr Pullings not three weeks past that them barrels was sweating something chronic down in the hold, but would anyone listen to old Killick? No sir, they would not, and now look at us, breeding them little black devils by the thousand, and half of them ready to fly off into the soup when a man's not looking... I've half a mind to report it to the Doctor, though fat lot of good that'll do when the Captain's got his mind on them French frigates and won't spare a moment for the provisions what keeps a ship sailing in the first place... Weevils in the biscuit again — which I suppose tomorrow they'll expect me to conjure up a decent supper out of sawdust and hope. Which I could have prevented it entire, had anyone bothered to ask my opinion before the ship left port, but there we are — Preserved Killick knows nothing, though thirty years at sea and a hundred voyages, and the only man aboard who remembers what proper stores looks like... Toast 'em, then? That's what the Doctor always says, very cheerful like, as if a man can make something wholesome out of corruption.

15 MARCH 1800

Which I have been to every dairy in this godforsaken place and there is not a drop of proper cream to be had, not a single drop, and all them slovenly Portuguese women tell me the same thing — too hot, too hot, it will not keep — as if I do not know that, as if I have not been steward in the Mediterranean these thirty years... The Captain's coffee, your honour, sitting there this very morning looking into his cup like a man who has lost his best friend, and what am I to offer him? Which it breaks my heart, it truly does, and all because of them lazy merchants who cannot be troubled... Might as well ask for the moon. Which there was cream aplenty in Port Royal, fresh and thick, but will anyone remember that when I am blamed for this morning's breakfast? They will not, sir, they will not.

14 MARCH 1800

Which I've only just got the Captain's quarters aired proper, and the sails are barely mended from them storm-torn canvas, and now they want me to conjure up berths like we're some sort of floating hotel for the Quality... Passengers. Which it always comes down to passengers, don't it, with all their trunks and their wanting hot water at hours that decent folk are asleep, and the linen never good enough though I bleached it myself in the sun... More guests for Captain's table, I suppose, which means I'll need to get out them plates we don't usually touch, the good Madeira's to be decanted — if there's any left after the Doctor's been at it — and the midshipmen will be tramping through my galley at all hours looking for something to eat when they ought to be at their books... Which the Captain never tells me nothing until the last moment, your honour.

13 MARCH 1800

Which I have darned these very stockings not three weeks past, and here they are again with a hole you could put your fist through, and the Captain going through 'em like them weevils go through the biscuit, which it fair wears a man down to think on it. A dozen pair a month, never less, and me with hardly a moment to call me own between the galley and the berth-deck and keeping them midshipmen from running riot through the cabins, and now here's the best merino — the best, mind you — ruined near about beyond salvation... Holes in the heels, always the heels. Which I ain't complaining, your honour would say I was, though the Lord knows I got more than enough cause, but a man does his duty and mends what needs mending, though I sometimes wonder if the Captain walks on the sharp edges of the world, the way these stockings give up the ghost. Not that I'd say nothing to him, which he's got enough to trouble his mind without worrying over his stockings, but still and all... The left foot worse than the right, which figures. 12 MARCH 1800

Which I saw them young gentlemen sneaking about with the Captain's own telescope — his best Dollond, mind you, not the common one — and never a word asked, never a by-your-leave, as though the ship was a toy-shop and they could help themselves to whatever took their fancy... The Captain prizes that glass above rubies, he does, and if so much as a speck of salt gets on them lenses there'll be the devil to pay, and who'll catch it? Not them midshipmen with their officers' privileges, I'll warrant. Which it'll be me that has to polish it out, me that has to answer for every scratch and smudge, and no thanks for it neither... The young swabs'll have it knocked about, dropped on the deck-planks, pointed at the sun like a parcel of school-boys... Which I've half a mind to report it to Mr Pullings, I have, though what good that'll do with them cocky young gentlemen I couldn't say, and besides the Captain's got enough vexations without me bringing him more troubles on a Wednesday morning when he's particular about his coffee... Little thieves. That's what they are.

❧

11 MARCH 1800

Which I have been at it since the forenoon watch, your honour, swabbing and bailing and trying to keep them leaks from the deckhead—not that anyone notices, mind you—and now here comes the Captain wanting his dinner like the weather don't signify, and me with naught but a damp cloth and a prayer to keep the soup from sloshing into his lap. The tablecloth's soaked through, the best one too, the damask I was saving for... Weevils wouldn't be so bad if the water didn't get at the stores. Which I've wedged the butter dish in the corner by the settee where it's least likely to go sliding across the cabin, but if that young midshipman don't stop lurching about like a sack of ballast, that'll be no manner of good, and then who'll get blamed when the cream's all over the deck? Not him, I assure you. Not him. The Doctor says to me this morning—says to me, mind you, without so much as a by-your-leave—he says, Killick, fetch me some hot water for my cabin. Hot water! Which I haven't slept in thirty-six hours, but never mind that. Which it'll settle soon enough. They always do.

❧

10 MARCH 1800

Which I've been at this plate for near on three hours, getting them spots out that nobody but me would even notice, my fingers worn raw with the cloth and the rouge, and does the Captain so much as remark on it when he takes his breakfast? Not a word. Not so much as a nod. That's the thanks you get for keeping the finest cabin in the Mediterranean, which I will say it without boasting, it shines like the very firmament, and them other captains' stewards, they don't hold a candle to... Mind, I do it for the ship, not for thanks. But a man might think, after all these years, all them long nights when my hammock's calling and I'm still here rubbing and polishing... Not that I'm complaining. Which I'm not.

9 MARCH 1800

Which I was only just arranging them ginger pieces, your honour's particular favourite, all nice and orderly in the tin, and now them vermin have got at 'em — gnawed right through the wrapping, they have, the thieving little... And the Captain will want his ginger for his coffee, won't he, and what am I to tell him? That the rats have been at it? As if I haven't enough to contend with, what with them weevils in the flour and the damp getting to everything and nobody giving me the least bit of notice or consideration for... for... Which it's rats now, is it?

❧

8 MARCH 1800

Which I haven't had me head on a pillow for seventy-two hours, not seventy-two hours, your honour, and now it's toasted cheese, as if I ain't got enough on me plate with them midshipmen tracking salt water through the gun-room and the Captain's Madeira wanting decanting and the galley-fire acting up something chronic, and toasted cheese don't make itself, no sir, it requires... it requires a certain touch, a certain delicacy that comes from experience, which I got in abundance but not from standing upright when I can barely keep me eyes... Aye aye, sir. Toasted cheese coming directly.

❧

7 MARCH 1800

Which I only just got them candlesticks polished not two hours ago, and now his honour wants the whole cabin turned inside out like a woman in a fit, and where I'm supposed to find them fresh flowers at sea beats me entirely, and the tablecloth that don't have a stain on it is in the wash because certain persons spilled their claret last Tuesday, so I'll have to use the second-best which shows every mark of salt spray and dust, and no one thinks to give Killick any notice whatsoever, not so much as a by-your-leave... Which them visitors won't know the difference anyway, sir or madam, not really. His honour will have it all perfect though, I grant him that. The Captain knows what's what. But the work, the endless work, and not a soul to help but young William, who couldn't dust a shelf without raising half the ship's motes... No notice. That's the way of it.

❧

6 MARCH 1800

Which I wouldn't mind it so much if there was another to be had, but which this here is the best linen in the ship and it wants washing before the Captain takes his dinner, and there's no time at all... Blast them midshipmen and their clumsy fingers, or it could be they've been at the wine again, one of 'em, couldn't keep a clean cloth for five minutes together if their lives depended on it... A stain. After all I done to keep it fresh and folded proper. Which I'll have to scrub it in salt water and hope to God it comes out, because if the Captain sees so much as a spot on his table there'll be... well, there won't be words for it, not that he'd raise his voice, but I'd rather face a broadside than that look he gets when things aren't... Ship-shape. That's the word. Them weevils never cause me half this much trouble.

5 MARCH 1800

Which I laid out them bottles not two hours past, and now there's a full inch gone from the Captain's best—his *best*, mark you—and not a word, not a whisper of explanation. Some thieving article with more thirst than sense, and me to answer for it when the Captain calls for his evening glass. As if I haven't enough on my plate, what with the midshipmen leaving their filthy boots in the forward cabin and them weevils getting into everything... The Captain trusted me with that Madeira, he did, and now... Fifty years at sea, and still they treat me like I'm made of stone. Which I shall have to tell him direct, I suppose, and take the blame though it weren't my doing—never is, though you'd think a man might show some gratitude for... Ungrateful dogs, the lot of them.

4 MARCH 1800

Which I have heard them Portuguese women wailing in the night, and I have heard the bosun's call on a calm morning, but nothing—nothing, your honour—has prepared the human ear for the Doctor's notion of music. It is as though some great beast is being murdered most slow, stretched out and tormented, and all for the sake of what he calls Culture. Which I do not doubt he means well, but the man cannot play for toffee, and that is a fact. The Captain does not complain, of course. But then the Captain is a gentleman of uncommon forbearance.

3 MARCH 1800

Which I have been at this coat these two hours with the flat iron, and them creases won't come proper, and the Captain wanting it for the dinner at the Governor's, and if there's a mark on it—which there won't be, not while I'm drawing breath—but if there was, it would be Killick's fault, not the damp in this infernal cabin or the ship's constant rolling, oh no... The iron's gone cold again. Of course it has. Which I ironed this very coat before Port Royal, and a fine job it was too, and now look at it—them threads coming loose at the shoulder seam, which I'll have to stitch before he notices, and where's the time, where's the time... Threads, wrinkles, and the Captain needing to look respectable while I'm down here sweating like a galley slave over a flat iron that won't hold heat.

2 MARCH 1800

Which I've told Mr Pullings a dozen times if I've told him once, that mill wants seeing to, but does anyone listen to old Killick? No sir, they do not. Now here's the Captain wanting his coffee, proper coffee, not them grounds floating about like... like them weevils in the biscuit, and what am I to do without a mill? Pound it with the mortar and pestle, I suppose, which'll take the better part of the morning and leave me no time for nothing else, the tablecloths won't lay themselves out, and the coffee'll be cold before it reaches his honour's cup, which is just the way of things aboard this vessel when a man tries his best...

1 MARCH 1800

Which I have not had a moment's peace since we came aboard, what with them weevils in the biscuit and the Captain wanting his coffee at all hours and the midshipmen trailing crumbs through the cabin like they was born in a barn, and now them weevils is got into the very best stores that I laid down myself with me own two hands, and there ain't nothing for it but to sift the whole lot through and pick 'em out one by one, which will take me until... until I don't know when, and nobody thinks to give Killick a word of thanks for keeping this ship's mess decent, no sir, they do not... Them weevils is the Devil's own invention, they are. Which I might as well be stirring porridge for the gun crews as try to serve a proper biscuit when it's alive with the little vermin, and the Captain will notice if his breakfast ain't just so, Captain notices everything, though I doubt he notices that *I* ain't slept above four hours in a fortnight on account of... The Doctor says they is good protein. Good protein! I'll give him good protein, sir, I will. Which there wasn't a weevil in this biscuit when I stowed it, your honour, not one, so either the stores was rotten to begin with—which would be a scandal

28 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I have been all over this damnable town asking for cream—cream, sir, not that thin skimmed stuff they flog to the ignorant—and every Portuguese housewife and her mother looks at me as though I had asked for the moon itself, which I might as well have done for all the good it does me. The Captain's coffee at eight bells, his honour particular about it, and here I am like some common swab begging at kitchen doors... Not a drop to be had. Not one blessed drop. Which it is all very well for them that sits ashore in their comfortable establishments with their nice fresh cows and their dairies, but I'll have the Captain looking at his cup with that expression he gets when things are not as they ought to be, and it will be my hide for it, though I've worn out three pair of shoes this day alone searching. They don't understand, none of them do. Not the Doctor with his head in his books, not Mr Pullings—good soul though he is—not one of them grasps that coffee without proper cream is just hot disappointment in a cup, and the Captain deserves better than that. Deserves the best, which I will find or know the reason why... Though at this rate I shall be seeking it till the next tide, and fat lot of good that will do when we've weighed anchor by then.

27 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I have barely had time to swab the orlop and now they're wanting berths made up for them lubbers, and which the Captain said nothing about it till just this moment, and the Doctor's got his sick-bay full of them specimens in bottles that takes up half the space, and now I'm to find room for... for... *mutters while walking away* More passengers. As if this ship weren't crowded enough with them that's meant to be here. Which I suppose it'll be the best linen they'll be wanting, the good damask that I was keeping for... but no matter, it's always the passengers that gets the best, and the crew can make do with the worn stuff, and which I'll be up half the night airing them berths out proper, and not a word of thanks for it neither. They never thinks about the work, do they sir, not the Captain nor nobody else. Which I had a plan for them stores, a neat arrangement it was, and now the whole thing's to be shifted about again, and them weevils will get into everything in the confusion, mark my words...

26 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I have darned these stockings till there ain't hardly a sound thread left in 'em, and when I take 'em to the Captain this evening he'll not say a word—not one word of thanks, your honour—just pull 'em on and walk about the quarterdeck like they mended themselves. Eighteen pair I've got in the mending basket this week alone, and him wearing through 'em like they was tissue paper, and all the while I'm meant to... I'm meant to be seeing to the stores and the silver and keeping them midshipmen from stealing the cream... Stockings don't grow on trees, they don't. Which a man might think the Captain would comprehend that these things require time and attention, and my hands ain't getting any younger neither, but there—there's another hole in the heel and I never even got to the toe yet... Nothing for it but to sit here by the lamp till my eyes go dark, stitching and stitching while the rest of the ship sleeps... The Doctor's got a pair that's lasted him good ten months without so much as a darn. Ten months. And him walking about in them all over creation.

Them middies wouldn't know proper care if it bit 'em on the... Which the Captain will want it this evening for certain, he always does, and here I'll be left explaining how his own officers couldn't be bothered to ask. They'll only stare at you blank-like and say Mr Pullings said they could, or some such taradiddle. I'll find it before the Captain gets wind of it, but they won't hear a civil word from me about it, not one. Let 'em wonder why there's no extra grog tonight.

The midshipmen's mess can have cold salt beef. I ain't risking the Captain's tablecloth. Which I told young Mr. Williamson not to touch nothing, but would he listen? 23 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I have been at this here silver since before dawn, and not a word—not so much as a by-your-leave—from anyone aboard this ship, and the Captain's guests tonight expecting it to shine like the blessed sun itself, which it will, because I am a professional in my line, but will they remark upon it? Will they hell. They'll drink his Madeira and eat off them plates without a thought to the hands that polished every last spoon, and tomorrow the Captain will find a spot—there's always a spot, some bit of tarnish I missed in the dark, the dark because there ain't no proper light in the pantry—and then there'll be words, oh yes, then Killick will hear about it... Them midshipmen couldn't shine a piece of plate if their lives depended on it. Which I do all the work aboard this vessel and no one knows it, and if something goes wrong, well, then they knows it quick enough, your honour gets told directly, and Killick gets the rough edge of the tongue. But when everything's proper and shipshape and running like clockwork, well, that's just as it should be, ain't it, nobody needs thanking for doing their duty... Not even a cup of decent coffee this morning.

22 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I took them tins up myself not three days ago, and them rats—them great whiskered vermin—they've gnawed clean through the copper and got at every last piece, and now what am I to say to his honour when he calls for his ginger with his coffee, which he particular likes it, and there's nothing for it but shame and... The Doctor won't be best pleased neither, seeing as he had designs on that ginger for his sick-bay preparations, or so he said, though likely he meant to filch a bit for himself, same as the rest of 'em. But that ain't the point. The point is them rats knew, they knew somehow, and they've made a right mess of the Captain's stores and there's nothing left but tins all holed and ginger spread about like... Nothing for it now but traps. Lots of traps. And no sleep.

21 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I haven't closed me eye since Tuesday last, your honour, and now you're wanting toasted cheese. Toasted cheese. As if there ain't the Captain's breakfast to lay out, and them weevils got into the ship's biscuit again, and the coffee's near gone because somebody — and I won't say names but he wears a coat with buttons — has been helping himself to the cream like it grows on trees. But no matter, I'll do your toasted cheese. I always do. Though how a man is meant to work the galley stove when he's dead on his feet and the cheese has gone hard as oakum, and I've got to find the good bread and not them mouldy ends... *trails off into muttering* The thanks I get for it, you could write on a grain of salt.

20 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I only this moment finished swabbing of them boards, and now here comes word the Captain wants the great cabin made ready for them commodore and his suite, and I've scarce had time to draw breath, let alone fetch up them good glasses from the stores, and the tablecloth — which I had particular in mind the damask, the one without them spots — it's only just come from the laundry and ain't properly aired, and if there's one thing I cannot abide it's damp linen, it shows a want of respect, and now I'm to get fresh flowers, which flowers, your honour, I should like to know, when we're three weeks from any land with green things growing... Which I shall do it, mind you. The Captain's word is law, and them visitors shall find the cabin shipshape or I'll know the reason why. But it's the notice, sir — that's what grinds me. A man might accomplish wonders with time enough, but this... Where in God's name has young Oakes got to with them candlesticks?

19 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I have took the greatest care with this cloth, your honour would not believe the labour, and now some villain has gone and... Lord, what is this? A stain? A stain! On the best damask, the very best, which I laid it out particular for them Portuguese ladies, and now it is ruined, utterly ruined, and not a soul aboard this barky gives a fig for the work that goes into keeping standards, not a single fig, and I shall have to... Christ, it will not come out, will it... That would be Bonden, mark my words. Or one of them young midshipmen with their filthy paws.

18 FEBRUARY 1800

Which I have been aboard this ship these twenty years and never seen such wickedness, which that Madeira cost the Captain's own money and it was decanted particular into them crystal glasses that I polished myself not two days since, and now some thieving article has... which I'll not say who, but there's one or two of them young gentlemen what thinks the world owes 'em a living, and if his honour finds out before I've had time to... well, there'll be the devil to pay and no mistake, and here's me without so much as a by-your-leave or a thank-you-kindly for keeping of his affairs in order.
